Other Regional Kingdoms (14th–18th Century)

Reference: Lucent GK, NCERT Class 6–12

These kingdoms played a crucial role in resisting foreign rule and preserving local culture, art, and architecture.

Rajput Kingdoms

  • Dominated Rajasthan and parts of North India
  • Famous dynasties: Sisodias of Mewar, Rathores of Marwar
  • Rana Sanga: Fought Babur in Battle of Khanwa (1527)
  • Maharana Pratap: Resisted Akbar in Battle of Haldighati (1576)

Ahom Kingdom

  • Located in Assam
  • Ruled for 600 years (1228–1826)
  • Famous for resisting Mughal invasions, especially during Battle of Saraighat (1671) under Lachit Borphukan

Sikh Confederacy / Punjab Kingdom

  • Founded by Guru Gobind Singh, militarized Sikhs into Khalsa
  • Later led by Maharaja Ranjit Singh (1799–1839):
    • Formed powerful Sikh Empire
    • Captured Lahore (capital)
    • Modernized army
    • Signed Treaty of Amritsar (1809) with British
    • Died in 1839 → Empire weakened → Anglo-Sikh Wars → annexed in 1849

Kingdom of Mysore

  • Ruled by Wodeyars, later taken over by Hyder Ali and Tipu Sultan
  • Tipu Sultan ("Tiger of Mysore") resisted British in Anglo-Mysore Wars
  • Died in Battle of Seringapatam (1799)

Kerala / Malabar Kingdoms

  • Dominated by Zamorins of Calicut
  • Famous for trade with Arabs, Chinese, and Europeans
  • Kunjali Marakkars: Naval chiefs who resisted Portuguese

Assam (Kamata & Koch Kingdoms)

  • Kamata Kingdom ruled western Assam
  • Koch Dynasty rose in the 16th century, later split into Koch Bihar and Koch Hajo

Bengal Nawabs

  • Declared independence from Mughals after Aurangzeb
  • Famous rulers: Murshid Quli Khan, Alivardi Khan, Siraj-ud-Daulah
  • Defeated by British in Battle of Plassey (1757)
